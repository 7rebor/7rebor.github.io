Kevin Dean (kevin.dean@utsouthwestern.edu)
2021-01-13 22:35:37

@Neil Anthony - We typically use sub-diffraction fluorescent beads that are cast in a 1% agarose cube for many of our alignment tasks, with the cube oriented such that the light is delivered and collected normal to the faces. We also try to minimize the depth at which we image into the cube since this minimizes spherical aberration. This is how we typically see the beam waist, and measure the performance, of a light-sheet. Putting the beads on a coverslip avoids refraction/spherical aberrations, but can be very confusing. Likewise, if you are dithering your lattice beam, or using a cylindrical lens to create your light-sheet, working in fluorescent solutions can also be confusing. When we do this, we simply use fluorescein.
