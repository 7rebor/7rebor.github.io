Sample prep for Leica SP8 DLS multi-view tissue using rotation mount

Just sharing a protocol with the community. I have been playing around with sample mounting on the Leica SP8 DLS. I needed to image some cleared mouse eyes (see #clearing channel for more conversation on that).

I just put this protocol together from my notes and photos. If these messages end up lasting forever, I hope this resource is useful for somebody in the future üôÇ

I've attached some example data of nuclear labelling (non-specific) to the end of the protocol and also here, we just want to identify the cells in the inner layer (LUT adjusted in last image). The data is good with the 5x/0.15 NA objective imaging in CUBIC-2 with the BABB/Gly mirrors. My biggest problems now are bubbles caught in the messy tissue and maybe some inconsistencies in the CUBIC solution, although the recommendation was to use Cargille Type FF immersion oil, I feel it is a waste.

For those not familiar, the DLS (digital light sheet) is an add-on to the SP8 confocal that mounts a detection objective opposite (above) the normal inverted objective turret used for confocal. The illumination comes from the bottom objective to two tiny mirrors attached to the detection objective, this turns the beam 90 degrees and perpendicular to the detection objective for a lightsheet (when scanned). Sample sizes can be up to 4 mm wide (parallel to the sheet) and 4 mm thick (axial), and quite long (cm? not very limited in that direction).
