*Thread Reply:* Hi Ana, I totally see your concerns, especially with the 20x objective with the adjustable collar ring for imaging samples in clearing solutions. We have been using it for imaging in ethyl cinnamate and EZ View solution (mainly containing urea and nycodenz, RI adjustable around 1.50 to 1.512). The objective is cleaned after each imaging session. The rubber O-rings do deteriorate over time, so I would check for leakage before and after each imaging session (if not during), regardless of what the imaging medium is.

Sunny (keqingdai@gmail.com)
2025-03-17 15:26:06

*Thread Reply:* I found these slides from Dr. Klein back in 2022 regarding the LightsheetZ.1 system (page 9 - 11), stating that fumes from organic solutions (BABB and DBE were listed, but this was before ECi became popular) might damage the motors and potentially the glue in the dipping lenses; formamide as well, "will damage dipping objectives". Not sure if anything is different now in the Lightsheet7, but based on these I would stick with ECi instead of BABB or DBE, and also clean up the dipping lenses as soon as imaging is finished.
