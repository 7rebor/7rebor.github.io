*Thread Reply:* Hi Kirstin, with our Lavision UMII imaging in RIMS (Sorbitol 70 % o r Histodenz) has been always very tricky with some positive acquisition (RIMS stable) that I can`t really explain; the UMII alignment tool was basically unusable; what I was doing was: avoid using the UMII alignment cuvette, but just aligning the beams on the cleared sample; My guess is that the stability (and optical homogeneity) of the solution can be improved by keeping the temperature constant, use fresh solutions, avoid evaporation and pre-incubate the sample in the imaging medium (to avoid mismatch between storage/clearing solution an imaging solution); otherwise, more drastic, changed the RIMS with a medium that does not mix, more stable and with same RI (like mix of mineral/silicon oil) – like in the CUBIC paper (I can send you reference if needed) or even more challenging, place the sample in small glass cuvette filled with RIMS (to keep it more stable) and fill the large cuvette with a stable medium (oil or others - similar config to what done with the MesoSPIM); that in theory should work, but as Peter told me..the 2 glass cuvette-walls should be as parallel as possible; hope helps..
