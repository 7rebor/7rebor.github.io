*Thread Reply:* Great, thanks all! Also looping in @Luca Verger and @Peter Gordon since (I think) they were replying to this post.

The workshop format is a series of technical presentations, so not much hands-on work unfortunately. If we wanted to do that, we'd have to set up a satellite workshop next to the conference, which is beyond my capabilities at least.

More guidelines: ```Technical workshops

A proposal must include from 4 (minimum) to 6 (maximum) speakers for a 3-hours workshop. Workshops are chaired by 1 or 2 persons who are responsible for leading the discussion during the session. It is recommended that no more than 1 chairperson also act as one of the speakers.``` I'd suggest covering clearing, imaging, and data analysis, as all three are likely to be (sequential) sticking points in a brain-clearing project. Maybe under the title of something like this? "Seeing everything, all at once: Brain Clearing, Light-sheet Imaging and Volumetric Data Analysis"

If we have 3 hours, we could plan with 3 blocks (Clearing, Light-Sheet Imaging, Data Analysis), 2 speakers each, 20 min + 10 min discussion per speaker. What do you think, does this make sense or does it not leave enough room for in-depth detail? Happy to hear your thoughts 🙂
